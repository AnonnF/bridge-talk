The students who couldn’t speak up, and the app trying to change that

A new student-built tool gives Imperial students somewhere to practise difficult conversations before they happen for real.

By The Imperial Standard · Student Life · 10 June 2026

Maya had been rehearsing the conversation in her head for two weeks. When the moment finally came, she said nothing. She left, sat on the steps outside Huxley, and felt the familiar weight of another missed opportunity. "Knowing isn't the same as doing," she said.

Her experience is not unusual. A survey of Imperial students this term found that over half regularly avoid conversations they know they need to have, whether with tutors, housemates, or potential employers.

How BridgeTalk works
Learn

Short multiple-choice lessons introduce communication frameworks at your own pace.

Practise

Peer-matching connects students anonymously for low-stakes, human-to-human conversations.

Reflect

A guided journal prompts reflection after interactions and tracks growth over time.

For friends & peers

Flatmates, project partners, and social circles experience less unresolved tension when students can navigate difficult conversations rather than avoid them.

For the university

Reduced pressure on overstretched counselling services, stronger student retention, and fewer escalations to crisis support.

Beyond Imperial

The learn, practise, reflect model is transferable to any university, workplace, or healthcare setting where people need to prepare for difficult conversations.
